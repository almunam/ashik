Rating Discrepancies & Consumer Trust

How multi-platform hotel reviews shape booking intentions — and what AI-generated content is doing to the trust infrastructure of the hospitality industry.

Md Ashikur Rahman
NF1000759
MA Digital Media & Global Communications
University of Niagara Falls Canada

Research Question

To what extent do multi-platform rating discrepancies function as a barrier to consumer trust, and how does the presence of synthetic (AI) content complicate the user's interpretive framework?

SQ1: Structural & algorithmic factors behind rating divergence  ·  SQ2: AI & fake review contribution to distrust  ·  SQ3: Management response effects on credibility

Methodology

Design-Based Secondary Research

A qualitative, secondary research design within a design-based framework. No original data collection — synthesises existing knowledge into accessible outputs.

01 Systematic Literature Review — 15+ peer-reviewed sources (2015–2025) via Google Scholar, EBSCO, Scopus.
02 Secondary Content Analysis — manual rating comparison for 5 Niagara Falls hotels across all three platforms. Illustrative.
03 Design-Based Production — six multimedia deliverables via iterative prototyping and peer review.
04 Thematic Analysis — three themes: platform structure, fake/AI content, management responses.

Key Finding — SQ2

137%

Sub-Question 3 — Management Responses

Response quality determines trust recovery

Key Finding — SQ3

Independent

Management responses have independent effects on consumer trust — over and above the original review content. Specific, accountable responses with a remedy partially offset even severe negative reviews.

Recommendations

Practical Guidance

For Consumers
  • Cross-check all three platforms before booking — never rely on a single source
  • Look for specificity: real reviews name staff, rooms, dates, and specific incidents
  • Treat suspiciously generic or perfectly worded reviews as potential AI-generated content
  • Read management responses as a proxy for hotel culture and accountability
For Hospitality Professionals
  • Respond within 24 hours — specifically and personally, not with copy-paste apologies
  • Monitor all three platforms as separate reputation channels, not one aggregate score
  • Report suspected fake reviews via platform dispute tools; document patterns over time
  • Train front-line staff to solicit genuine post-stay feedback at checkout
For Platforms & Researchers
  • Make AI-detection confidence signals visible to consumers at point of reading
  • Standardise verification disclosure and reviewer identity signals across platforms
  • Invest in cross-platform calibration tools so consumers can compare ratings fairly
  • Further empirical research needed on consumer experience of cross-platform conflict
