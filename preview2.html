AI-Generated Review Rate on TripAdvisor (2019–2024)

Originality.AI (2024) analysed TripAdvisor reviews using its proprietary AI detection tool across 20 major North American cities and five business categories. The data show a non-linear pattern: a dip following 2019, a modest recovery through 2022, then a sharp 2024 increase that coincides with the wider adoption of generative AI tools.

TripAdvisor Fake Review Removals (2024)

What Was Removed & Why

Of 2.7 million fraudulent reviews removed in 2024 (TripAdvisor, 2025):

Review boosting (businesses inflating own ratings) 54%
AI-generated content (214,000 reviews) ~8%
Other fraudulent activity (paid reviews, sabotage) ~38%

Source: TripAdvisor Transparency Report (2025). AI % is approximate — 214,000 of 2.7M total.

Industry Response

2022

ChatGPT launches — accessible AI lowered the barrier to generating realistic review-style text at scale.

2023

Coalition for Trusted Reviews formed — TripAdvisor, Booking.com, Amazon, Expedia, Glassdoor, and Trustpilot define best practices, share fake-review detection methods, and support stronger review integrity efforts (TripAdvisor, 2025).

2024

TripAdvisor removes 2.7 million fraudulent reviews — up from 2 million the year before. AI-generated removals reach 214,000.

2025+

Detection remains difficult. Wu et al. (2020) note that detection methods can report high accuracy, yet their real-world effect on reducing fake reviews remains limited.

How Fake Reviews Affect Consumer Trust

🔍

Platform-Level Trust Erosion

When consumers detect or suspect fake reviews, trust does not fall only for the fraudulent item — it degrades across the entire platform. A spillover effect undermines authentic reviews alongside fabricated ones (Pena-Garcia et al., 2024).

⚖️

Review Adoption Breakdown

Filieri et al. (2015) found that perceived platform trustworthiness is a precondition for acting on reviews. Once that trust falls below a threshold, consumers become reluctant to rely on any review — real or fabricated.

🏨

Hotelier Reputation Risk

Both competitor sabotage (negative fake reviews) and review boosting (inflated positive reviews) distort the information environment, creating unfair competitive conditions and undermining the credibility of the entire sector (Wu et al., 2020).

Research Synthesis

The proliferation of AI-generated reviews is not merely a volume problem — it is a trust infrastructure problem. Wu et al. (2020) documented that many detection methods report high accuracy, but their real-world effect on reducing fake reviews remains limited. Pena-Garcia et al. (2024) confirmed the downstream consequence: when consumers perceive fakeness, trust can decline for rating systems and the platforms hosting them. The 137% estimated increase documented by Originality.AI (2024) suggests this risk may grow, making platform-level transparency and consumer media literacy increasingly critical.
